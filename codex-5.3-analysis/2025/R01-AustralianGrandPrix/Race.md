**Round:** 1

---

## Session Summary

Lando Norris won a chaotic season opener in 1:42:06.304, beating Max Verstappen by 0.895s after multiple weather and safety-car phase changes. The race was defined less by pure stint pace and more by crossover judgment, restart execution, and error control in rapidly changing grip.

The start procedure was disrupted immediately when Isack Hadjar crashed on the formation lap, triggering an aborted start. After the eventual launch, early incidents for Jack Doohan and Carlos Sainz brought a safety car, and the field settled into a long intermediate-tyre phase.

Mid-race, Fernando Alonso crashed and caused another safety car, splitting the field between early slick-switch attempts and teams that stayed conservative. A second rain hit late, forcing another crossover window. Norris and Oscar Piastri both went off in the same phase; Norris recovered and made the right stop sequence, while Piastri lost major time and fell out of podium contention.

Late crashes for Liam Lawson and Gabriel Bortoleto produced a final safety-car restart. Norris held Verstappen through the closing laps, while George Russell and rookie Kimi Antonelli finished P3 and P4 (Antonelli’s 5s unsafe-release penalty was rescinded after review).

## Cascading Effects

Hadjar’s formation-lap crash caused an aborted start and reset procedures. The early Doohan/Sainz incidents produced a safety-car phase that compressed tyre degradation and strategic divergence timing.

Alonso’s crash triggered the mid-race safety car and opened the first major crossover decision window. Late crashes by Lawson and Bortoleto generated another safety car, neutralizing gaps and creating a final restart shootout that directly shaped podium margins.

Antonelli’s 5s unsafe-release penalty initially dropped him in classification, but stewards rescinded it after review, altering final official order and Mercedes points haul.
